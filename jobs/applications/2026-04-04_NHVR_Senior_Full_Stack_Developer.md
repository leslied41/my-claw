## Job Description
About the role
Working as Senior Full Stack Developer – Access and GIS Solutions you will be responsible for the design, build and maintenance of high‑quality full‑stack solutions that support NHVR's access and GIS platforms. You will ensure solutions meet technical, functional and performance standards as well as contribute to testing and quality assurance.
Key responsibilities of this role are:

Design, develop, and maintain high-quality, scalable web applications using LoopBack (Node.js) and React using maintainable, well documented code.
Implement and manage microservice architecture, ensuring each service is optimised for performance and reliability as well as deploy applications on Azure, leveraging its services for optimal performance.
Develop and integrate RESTful APIs and ensure secure data transactions.
Troubleshoot and resolve issues related to application performance, security, and scalability.
Participate in code reviews and contribute to the continuous improvement of the development process.
Design and manage databases, including MySQL and PostgreSQL, ensuring efficient data storage and retrieval.
Define and implement JSON Schema for data validation and API documentation.
Stay updated with emerging technologies and industry trends to enhance our technology stack continuously.

NHVR's Data & Technology team are currently undertaking growth and considerable transformation, and this is an exciting time to join the organisation.
To learn more, the position description can be found on the NHVR Careers site - https://careers.nhvr.gov.au/jobs/search

Why join us?
The National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR) commenced operations in 2013 as the national entity responsible for regulating all vehicles in Australia over 4.5 tonnes gross vehicle mass. The NHVR minimises the compliance burden on the heavy vehicle transport industry, reduces duplication and inconsistences across state and territory borders, and ultimately for the heavy vehicle business with government in Australia.
We are headquartered in Brisbane and employ more than 1000 people across the ACT, New South Wales, Queensland, South Australia, Tasmania and Victoria.
