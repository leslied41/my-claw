## Job Description
About Orygen   
Orygen is Australia's Centre of Excellence in Youth Mental Health and the largest translational research organisation dedicated to youth mental health in the world.  For over 30 years, Orygen has led groundbreaking early intervention and developed evidence-based models of care to improve the mental health outcomes of young people aged 12-25.   
About the job   Every day, young people across Australia turn to MOST for support — whether in crisis, taking their first step or checking in with a clinician — and the experience they have is shaped by the engineers who build it.   
As a Full Stack Engineer at Orygen Digital, you'll deliver end‑to‑end features across backend, API and frontend systems, partner closely with product, design, research and clinical teams, and contribute to shared engineering standards and your own structured professional growth.   
MOST has evolved from a small research project into a national youth mental health service, and while we've strengthened our foundations through improved testing, CI/CD, infrastructure and on-call practices, there is more to do — and this role is central to that next stage.  
In this role you will:  Develop and own features across the full stack, from backend services and APIs to frontend interfaces, ensuring they are tested, maintainable, and something you would be proud to hand to the next engineer. Work as an active partner to product, design, research, and clinical colleagues, translating complex requirements into reliable, accessible, and performant user experiences for young people and health professionals. Contribute to the Engineering Chapter, helping shape and uphold the shared standards, practices, and technical direction that raise the capability of the whole engineering organisation. Handle sensitive clinical and personal data with care, ensuring systems comply with healthcare regulations, privacy obligations, and accreditation standards. Participate in a compensated on-call rotation, performing triage and incident response to keep MOST available for the young people and clinicians who depend on it.  

Skills and experience   
The following criteria must be met for consideration for this position:  

Strong command of Python and/or PHP, with demonstrable experience building scalable, maintainable, and performant server-side applications in a production environment. Solid familiarity with RESTful API design and database capability across relational stores such as MariaDB or PostgreSQL. Confident working with CI/CD pipelines, containerisation (Docker), and cloud-native services, with practical AWS experience preferred. Solid frontend capability in Vue.js, JavaScript/TypeScript, HTML, and CSS, sufficient to own full-stack feature delivery end to end. Clear, honest communicator, written and verbal, able to articulate technical trade-offs to both engineering and non-engineering stakeholders.  

Benefits  $110,000 - $135,000 commensurate with skills and experience + 12% superannuation and $15,900 in NFP salary packaging + $2,600 meals and entertainment card. Fully remote position, work from wherever you do your best work. If you are in Melbourne, our Parkville office is always open for face-to-face time with the team whenever you want it. A structured professional development framework built on the 70/20/10 model, combining hands-on delivery, peer learning, and dedicated budget for conferences, training, and external development. Work that has a direct, measurable impact on the lives of young people at some of the most vulnerable moments of their lives. Work alongside a team that has spent the last twelve months rebuilding engineering foundations with care, conviction, and craft, and is not done yet. Thrive in a supportive, inclusive culture that values diversity and promotes wellbeing. Ability to purchase additional leave; access to EAP; generous Parental Leave Scheme.
